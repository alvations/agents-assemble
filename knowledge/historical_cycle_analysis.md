# Historical Cycle Analysis for 2026

## 1. Which Historical Year Does 2026 Resemble?

2026 is a **midterm election year** in the **2nd year of a presidential term** -- historically the weakest of the four-year cycle. The S&P 500 Cycle Composite (blending seasonal, presidential, and decennial cycles from 1928-2024) projects: strong January-February, sharp decline through April, choppy summer, deep trough in **early-to-mid October**, then a powerful Q4 rally. The S&P 500 is currently down 4.3% YTD at ~6,582, already tracking the midterm weakness template.

**Closest analog years:** 1962 (Cuban Missile Crisis midterm + geopolitical shock), 1990 (Gulf War midterm + oil spike), 2002 (post-9/11 war midterm). All three featured H1 drawdowns of 15-28% followed by powerful recoveries.

**87% of midterm years end positive**, with average full-year returns of **+19%**. Average peak-to-trough decline: **-17.3% over 115 days** (late April to mid-August), followed by average +31.7% one-year forward returns from the trough.

**Prediction:** S&P 500 likely bottoms around 5,600-5,900 in August-October 2026, then rallies to 6,800-7,200 by year-end. **Trades:** Buy SPY/QQQ on any 10%+ drawdown. Sell VIX calls (hedge unwinding) in October.

## 2. Oil Spike Historical Comparisons

Current situation: Brent crude surged from $76 (Jan) to $115+ (late March) -- a **51% spike** driven by Operation Epic Fury and Strait of Hormuz disruption. ~1 billion barrels lost. Gas at $4.50+/gallon.

| Oil Shock | Price Move | S&P 500 Impact | Recovery Time |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| 1973 OPEC Embargo | $3 to $12 (4x) | -40% bear market | 2+ years |
| 1979 Iran Revolution | $14 to $40 (2.8x) | +18.4% (priced in) | N/A |
| 1990 Gulf War | $15 to $42 (+75%) | -20%, quick reversal | 6 months |
| 2008 Peak | $147 peak | -37% (recession) | 4 years |
| 2022 Russia-Ukraine | $130 peak | -19% (bear) | 12 months |

**Key insight:** Short disruptions (1990, 2022) resolved in months. Only sustained shocks + recession = prolonged damage. The 1979 analog is instructive: markets had already priced in energy risk.

**Prediction:** If Iran conflict stays contained (no full Hormuz closure), resembles 1990 -- oil spikes then reverses, stocks recover within 6 months. If prolonged, resembles 1973 -- recession risk rises sharply. **Trades:** Long XLE, XOP (energy up 34% YTD, momentum continues). Long XOM (already +42% YTD). Short consumer discretionary (XLY) as gasoline costs crush spending.

## 3. Midterm Election Year Seasonality

Historical pattern is remarkably consistent across 24 midterm cycles since 1926:

- **Jan-Sep:** Average cumulative decline of -2% to -8%
- **October:** Average loss of -3%, marking the cycle low
- **Nov-Dec:** Average gains of +4% and +2% respectively
- **Best six months:** November of midterm year through April of pre-election year averages **+16.4% (unannualized)** since 1926
- **12 months post-midterm:** Average S&P 500 return of **+12.4%** vs 8.9% long-run average

**Prediction:** Expect continued weakness through September 2026. The Nov 2026 - Apr 2027 window is historically the single strongest six-month period in the entire four-year cycle. **Trades:** Accumulate broad market positions (SPY, QQQ, IWM) in August-October. Maximum bullish positioning by November 1. Consider LEAP calls on IWM (small caps benefit most from post-midterm rally).

## 4. Rate Cutting Cycle (12-24 Months In)

The Fed began cutting in September 2024 (50bp), followed by three more 25bp cuts. We are now ~18 months into the easing cycle.

**Historical data (11 cycles since 1970):**
- 12 months after first cut: median S&P 500 return **+11.9%**, average +7.6%
- Non-recession cuts: average **+17%** in 12 months (actual 2025 delivered +17.9%, right on target)
- 86% of the time, S&P 500 is positive 12 months after first cut

**Where we are now (months 13-24):** Returns in the second year of cutting cycles are more variable and depend heavily on whether the economy avoids recession. The 2025 return of +17.9% was 75% earnings-driven (13.5pp from EPS growth), which is the healthy kind. However, the Iran oil shock is a new risk that wasn't in the baseline.

**Prediction:** If no recession, the rate-cut tailwind suggests another +8-12% for 2026 from current levels. But oil above $100 for 6+ months historically triggers recession. **Trades:** Long financials (XLF) -- banks benefit from steeper yield curve. Long homebuilders (XHB, ITB) if rates resume falling. Avoid long-duration tech (ARKK) until rate path clarifies.

## 5. Defense Stock Rally Duration

**Historical defense rally durations:**

| Conflict | Defense Sector Rally | Duration of Outperformance |
|----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| Gulf War 1990-91 | Initial spike, then +28.8% broad market | 6-12 months |
| Afghanistan/Iraq 2001-03 | A&D index nearly **tripled** in 4 years | 3-4 years (sustained budgets) |
| Ukraine 2022+ | SPADE Defense Index +48% | 2+ years (ongoing) |
| Iran 2026 (current) | NOC +6%, RTX +4.7%, LMT +3.3% on Day 1 | TBD |

**Key finding:** Duration depends on fiscal commitment. Afghanistan/Iraq delivered the longest rally because budgets expanded for years. Current backlogs: RTX $268B, LMT $194B, GD $118B. Proposed 2027 defense budget: **$1.5 trillion**. Defense hiring surging (178K jobs in March, beating 65K estimate).

**Prediction:** This rally has multi-year legs given $1.5T budget proposals and munitions replenishment needs. The Afghanistan/Iraq analog (3-4 years of outperformance) is most relevant. **Trades:** Long LMT, NOC, RTX, GD. ETFs: ITA (iShares Aerospace & Defense), PPA (Invesco Aerospace & Defense). For munitions-specific exposure: LHX (L3Harris), AXON. Trim after any ceasefire announcement -- historically defense stocks give back 10-15% on peace news.
