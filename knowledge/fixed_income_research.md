# Fixed Income Research (2025)

## Current Environment
- Yield curve steepening (short rates falling faster than long rates)
- More Fed rate cuts expected → short-term yields falling further
- Long-term yields may remain rangebound or climb higher
- Result: short/intermediate duration outperforms

## Strategies for Rising Rate Protection
1. Floating-rate notes (FRNs)
2. TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected)
3. Short-duration ETFs (SHY, VCSH)
4. Active duration management

## Our Fixed Income Strategy Issues
- Current: -0.80 Sharpe on 3Y, -2.8% return
- Problem: overweight TLT when rates were rising in 2022
- Need: dynamic duration adjustment based on yield curve signals

## Improvement Ideas
1. Use 10Y-2Y spread from FRED (T10Y2Y) as regime signal
2. When curve inverted → go long TLT (rates will fall)
3. When curve steepening → go short duration (SHY/IEF)
4. Add TIPS for inflation protection
5. Use momentum on bond ETFs as additional filter

## Bond ETF Universe (K-1 safe)
- SHY: 1-3 year Treasury (no K-1)
- IEF: 7-10 year Treasury (no K-1)
- TLT: 20+ year Treasury (no K-1)
- TIP: TIPS inflation-protected (no K-1)
- LQD: Investment grade corporate (no K-1)
- HYG: High yield corporate (no K-1)
- BND: Total bond market (no K-1)
- AGG: Aggregate bond index (no K-1)
