# Cyclical & Seasonal Industry Trading Patterns

Research date: 2026-04-06. Sources: Stock Trader's Almanac, CME Group, USDA Extension, EIA, Barchart, Seeking Alpha, DrDuru.

## 1. Retail Stocks (Holiday/Q4 Cycle)

**Tickers:** AMZN, WMT, TGT, XRT (ETF)

| Action | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **BUY** | Late September - October | Pre-holiday dip; September avg return -0.75% (worst month since 1950) |
| **SELL** | Late December - early January | Santa Claus rally peaks; last 5 trading days Dec + first 2 Jan avg +1.3% (79% hit rate) |

- **Average seasonal return:** Nov-Dec S&P retail names +3-5%; AMZN Q4 2024 revenue $187.8B (first time surpassing WMT), earnings growth +86% YoY.
- **2024-2025:** Pattern HELD. Q4 2024 broadline retail earnings surged 71% YoY. Holiday retail sales +3.8% YoY.

## 2. Airline Stocks (Summer Travel Cycle)

**Tickers:** DAL, UAL, LUV, JETS (ETF)

| Action | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **BUY** | October - November | Post-summer demand trough; Sept-Nov weakest passenger volumes |
| **SELL** | May - June | Pre-summer pricing peak; capacity announcements priced in before Memorial Day |

- **Average seasonal return:** Q4 historically strongest quarter for airline equities (NYSE Arca Global Airlines Index). JETS avg monthly return +0.55%.
- **271M passengers** carried Jun-Aug 2024 (record). DAL 2024 revenue $61.6B (+6% YoY); UAL Q4 revenue $14.7B (+8% YoY).
- **2024-2025:** Pattern PARTIALLY HELD. Summer oversupply compressed margins despite record demand. Q4 strength confirmed with record profits at DAL and UAL. LUV lagging but expected +107% earnings growth 2025.

## 3. Agricultural Commodities (Planting/Harvest Cycle)

**Tickers/Futures:** ZC (corn), ZW (wheat), ZS (soybeans)

| Commodity | BUY (seasonal low) | SELL (seasonal high) | Cycle driver |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|--------------|
| **Corn** | Oct - Nov (harvest glut) | Jun - Jul (weather uncertainty) | Plant Apr-May, harvest Sep-Oct |
| **Soybeans** | Oct - Nov (harvest) | Jun - Aug (mid-summer peak) | Plant May-Jun, harvest Sep-Nov |
| **Winter Wheat** | Jul (harvest) | Feb - Mar (storage demand) | Plant Sep-Oct, harvest Jun-Jul |

- **Average seasonal move:** Corn bottoms at harvest 9 out of 10 years (UW Extension). Prices typically peak by July 4th -- "have stored grain marketing done by July 4th" (Extension rule of thumb).
- **Key disruption:** South American soybean harvest (Feb) heavily influences U.S. pricing. Weather markets May-Jul can swing 20%+ in either direction.
- **2024-2025:** Patterns broadly held but muted by strong global supply and record South American production.

## 4. Homebuilder Stocks (Spring Selling Season)

**Tickers:** DHI, LEN, TOL, ITB (ETF), XHB (ETF)

| Action | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **BUY** | October - November | Pre-spring selling season positioning |
| **SELL** | March (optimal) or May (latest) | Spring selling season peak; momentum fades after March 71% of years |

- **Average seasonal return:** Nov +1.4%, Dec +6.2%, Jan +6.4% vs S&P 500. 75% of annual homebuilder returns concentrated in Nov-Jan.
- **2024-2025:** Pattern BROKE. ITB suffered -16.5% in Dec 2024 (worst since pandemic March 2020). Worst seasonal start since GFC. High mortgage rates and affordability pressures overwhelmed seasonal tailwinds. XHB returned only 9.87% in 2024, down -0.70% in 2025.

## 5. Energy Stocks (Heating/Driving Cycle)

**Tickers:** XOM, CVX, XLE (ETF); natural gas: UNG (ETF)

### Crude Oil / Energy Equities
| Action | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **BUY** | Late August - September | Pre-winter heating demand buildup |
| **SELL** | April - May | Peak refinery demand fades after spring maintenance |

- XLE best month historically: February. Best sell month: September. April avg return +6.1%.
- Nov-Apr (XLE): outperforms May-Oct period; S&P 500 Nov-Apr avg +7.2% vs May-Oct +2.1%.

### Natural Gas (distinct cycle)
| Action | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **BUY** | April - May (injection season low) | Lowest prices of the year; storage refill begins |
| **SELL** | January - February (withdrawal season peak) | Peak heating demand; highest prices of the year |

- **Average seasonal spread:** Oct-to-Jan futures differential ~12% (CME/ICE data). Withdrawal season Nov-Mar; injection season Apr-Oct.
- **2024-2025:** Energy patterns held broadly. Natural gas prices spiked in winter 2024-25 on cold weather. Crude oil seasonal pattern muted by OPEC+ supply management and geopolitical volatility.

## 6. Back-to-School Consumer Stocks

**Tickers:** TGT, WMT, BBY, DLTR, DKS, AAPL (electronics)

| Action | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **BUY** | Late June - early July | Pre-spending ramp; July avg return +1.2% (4th strongest month) |
| **SELL** | Late August - early September | Spending peaks in August (39% of back-to-school spend); September avg return -0.75% |

- **Total spend:** $128.2B back-to-school 2025 (+2.23% YoY). K-12 avg $858/child.
- **Trend shift:** 51% of consumers shopping earlier (July vs August) due to tariff fears. Discount retailers (WMT, TGT, DLTR) outperforming as consumers trade down.
- **Sector winners 2024-2025:** Clothing stores +1.0%, sporting goods/bookstores +0.8% in August. Premium electronics (AAPL, BBY) benefiting from higher-income household demand.
- **2024-2025:** Pattern HELD with timing shift earlier into July.
