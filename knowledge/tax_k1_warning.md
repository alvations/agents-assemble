# K-1 Tax Form Warning: ETFs and MLPs

## Instruments that issue K-1 forms (NOT 1099-DIV)

These instruments complicate tax filing significantly. They issue Schedule K-1
instead of standard 1099-DIV forms, requiring partnership tax returns.

### Commodity ETFs (most common K-1 issuers)
- **USO** — United States Oil Fund (K-1)
- **UNG** — United States Natural Gas Fund (K-1)
- **DBA** — Invesco DB Agriculture Fund (K-1)
- **DBC** — Invesco DB Commodity Index (K-1)
- **DBB** — Invesco DB Base Metals (K-1)
- **WEAT** — Teucrium Wheat Fund (K-1)
- **CORN** — Teucrium Corn Fund (K-1)
- **GSG** — iShares S&P GSCI Commodity ETF (K-1)

### MLPs (Master Limited Partnerships)
- **EPD** — Enterprise Products Partners
- **ET** — Energy Transfer
- **MPLX** — MPLX LP
- **WES** — Western Midstream Partners

### Bitcoin/Crypto Futures ETFs
- **BITO** — ProShares Bitcoin Strategy (K-1 if futures-based structure)

## Safe Alternatives (1099-DIV, no K-1)
- **GLD/IAU** — Gold ETFs (grantor trusts, 1099-B)
- **SLV** — Silver ETF (grantor trust, 1099-B)
- **XLE/XOP** — Energy equity ETFs (standard 1099-DIV)
- **PDBC** — Invesco Optimum Yield Commodity (1099-DIV, NOT K-1!)
- **IBIT/FBTC** — Spot Bitcoin ETFs (1099, not K-1)

## Recommendation
For taxable accounts, prefer 1099-issuing alternatives. K-1 instruments are
fine in IRAs/401k where tax forms don't matter.
