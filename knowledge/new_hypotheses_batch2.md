## Hypothesis 1: Tariff-Driven Reshoring / US Manufacturing Renaissance

### Thesis
The US reshoring wave has moved from political rhetoric to real capital deployment. Over $3 trillion in reshoring-related investments have been announced since early 2025. A significant sector rotation is underway -- the Russell 2000 has outperformed the Nasdaq by ~9% over 30 days as of March 2026. Steel demand is forecast to grow 1.8-3.4% in 2026, driven by data center construction, power grid modernization, and EV factory buildouts. HRC steel spot prices breached $1,000/ton in April 2026.

### Tickers

**Primary (Long):**
- **NUE (Nucor)** -- Largest US steel producer. Analyst consensus Strong Buy (15 of 24 analysts). Median PT $180, high $200. Expected 19% earnings growth on 8.3% revenue growth in 2026. Investing $725M in Lafayette, IN engine manufacturing facility. Current price ~$163.
- **STLD (Steel Dynamics)** -- Up 30.6% on 6-month basis and 38.4% over 52 weeks. Second-largest EAF steelmaker. Benefits from same reshoring demand tailwinds as Nucor with faster growth profile.
- **CLF (Cleveland-Cliffs)** -- "Big Three" domestic steel. More leveraged play (higher beta) on US steel demand.
- **CAT (Caterpillar)** -- Zacks Rank #2 (Buy). Shifted production from Japan to Georgia/Texas. Expected 19% EPS growth on 8.3% revenue growth. Benefits from infrastructure construction boom.
- **ENS (EnerSys)** -- Moving battery production from Mexico to Kentucky, mitigating tariff risk. Niche reshoring beneficiary.

**Secondary (Long):**
- **SWK (Stanley Black & Decker)** -- Domestic manufacturing tools/industrial.
- **ETN (Eaton)** -- Electrical infrastructure for reshored factories + data centers (see Hypothesis 2).

### Entry/Exit Logic
- **Entry:** Buy on pullbacks to 20-day MA or on weakness following tariff policy announcements that create temporary fear (the policy direction is firmly pro-reshoring). NUE entry zone: $155-165.
- **Exit:** Take profits if steel HRC prices fall below $900/ton sustainably, or if a major trade deal removes tariff premiums. Trim 25% at analyst PT ($180 NUE, $200 STLD). Full exit if reshoring capex announcements decelerate for 2 consecutive quarters.
- **Stop loss:** 12% below entry for individual names.

### Expected Horizon
6-18 months. The reshoring capital cycle is multi-year but steel prices are cyclical -- the current $1,000+ HRC price likely represents near-peak profitability. Position for the structural trend but trade the cycle.

## Hypothesis 3: GLP-1 Disruption -- Short the Losers

### Thesis
GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic, Zepbound, Mounjaro) are the most disruptive force in consumer health in decades. ~10M Americans on branded GLP-1s in 2026, projected 30M+ by 2030. Prices are falling, oral versions arriving, Medicare coverage expanding. 70% of GLP-1 users report snacking less. EY-Parthenon estimates $12B dent in snack food market growth. Zepbound received FDA approval to treat obstructive sleep apnea -- the first-ever drug approved for OSA, directly threatening CPAP device makers. This is a SHORT thesis on companies whose revenues face structural headwinds.

### Tickers

**Short Candidates (or Underweight):**

*Snack Food / Packaged Food:*
- **HSY (Hershey)** -- Flagged softer demand. Pure-play confectionery with high exposure to impulse snacking. Most vulnerable category.
- **MDLZ (Mondelez)** -- Dropped 4.3% in sector sell-off. CEO estimated only 1-1.5% volume impact over 10 years, but market is pricing in more. Oreo/cadbury = impulse snacking.
- **KHC (Kraft Heinz)** -- Dropped 3.5%. Splitting up. Investing $600M to reinvent. Structural challenges beyond GLP-1 (pricing power fading).
- **GIS (General Mills)** -- Shares sank 7% on slashed 2026 outlook citing "weak consumer sentiment." High snack exposure (Nature Valley, Chex Mix).
- **PEP (PepsiCo)** -- Cutting prices on core US snack lines. Frito-Lay division faces volume pressure. Better diversified than pure snack plays.

*Medical Devices -- Sleep Apnea:*
- **RMD (ResMed)** -- Stock trading well below 52-week high (~$180). In "penalty box." Zepbound's OSA approval is a direct threat to CPAP demand. 70-90% of OSA driven by obesity. 9/11 analysts still rate Buy (PT $296), but the long-term thesis is impaired. The paradox: short-term CPAP prescriptions up as more patients see doctors, but 3-5 year demand at risk.
- **INSP (Inspire Medical)** -- Implantable sleep apnea device. Even more vulnerable than ResMed to weight-loss-driven OSA resolution.

*Medical Devices -- Bariatric Surgery:*
- **ISRG (Intuitive Surgical)** -- Bariatric procedure growth slowing as patients try GLP-1 first. However, company is highly diversified across surgery types -- this is a minor revenue headwind, not a short thesis on its own.

**Long the Winners (Pair Trade):**
- **LLY (Eli Lilly)** -- First pharma to surpass $1T market cap. Zepbound/Mounjaro franchise. The GLP-1 king.
- **NVO (Novo Nordisk)** -- Ozempic/Wegovy. Duopoly with Lilly.

### Entry/Exit Logic
- **Short entry (snack food):** Short HSY on rallies above $175 or GIS on rallies above $60. These are slow bleed trades, not crash trades. The 1-1.5% annual volume drag compounds.
- **Short entry (RMD):** Short on rallies toward $250+ or on any quarter where management claims GLP-1 is a "tailwind." The narrative will eventually flip.
- **Pair trade:** Long LLY / Short HSY or Long NVO / Short RMD. This neutralizes broad market risk.
- **Exit shorts:** Cover if GLP-1 adoption stalls (FDA safety warning, insurance pullback, major side effect discovery). Cover RMD if sleep apnea device volumes accelerate 2+ consecutive quarters despite GLP-1 adoption growth.
- **Stop loss:** 15% for shorts (these can squeeze on any "GLP-1 not so bad" headline).

### Expected Horizon
12-36 months. The volume impact is real but slow-moving. By 2028-2030 with 30M+ users, the revenue drag on snack food becomes undeniable. RMD/INSP face structural risk on a 3-5 year basis as oral GLP-1s dramatically expand the addressable patient population.

## Hypothesis 4: Aging Population Demographics -- Japan & Europe Healthcare

### Thesis

Europe: Germany's 65+ cohort rising from 22% to 30% by 2060. Global geriatric care services market valued at $1T in 2022, projected to double by 2033. Projected shortage of 2.3M long-term care workers by 2030 creates demand for automation/robotics.

### Tickers

**Japanese Healthcare / Medical Devices (Long):**
- **Olympus (TYO: 7733 / OTC: OCPNY)** -- 70%+ market share in gastrointestinal endoscopy. Partnering with NVIDIA for AI-powered cloud-connected endoscopes. Japan's aging population drives endoscopy demand (cancer screening).
- **Terumo (TYO: 4543)** -- Cardiovascular devices (stents, catheters). Direct beneficiary of aging-related cardiovascular disease. Founded 1921, deep Japan market penetration.
- **Sysmex (TYO: 6869 / OTC: SSMXY)** -- Clinical diagnostics. Acquired JEOL's medical equipment division (Sep 2025). Aging = more diagnostic testing.

**Global Aging Plays (Long):**
- **ISRG (Intuitive Surgical)** -- Robotic surgery adoption in Japan growing rapidly (da Vinci approved by MHLW). Aging patients need minimally invasive procedures. Addresses the surgeon shortage problem.
- **MDT (Medtronic)** -- Diversified medical devices. Diabetes, cardiovascular, surgical. Global aging exposure.
- **ABT (Abbott Labs)** -- Diagnostics + medical devices + nutrition. Balanced aging exposure.
- **SYK (Stryker)** -- Joint replacements, surgical equipment. Direct beneficiary of aging orthopedic demand.

**ETFs for Broader Exposure:**
- **AGNG (Global X Aging Population ETF)** -- Broad exposure to aging theme.
- **AGED (iShares Ageing Population UCITS ETF)** -- Developed + emerging market aging beneficiaries.
- **OLD (Long-Term Care ETF)** -- Senior living facilities, nursing, specialty hospitals.

**Automation / Robotics (Long):**
- **Worker shortage = automation demand.** Japan is pioneering healthcare robotics. ISRG is the Western play; Japanese robotics firms (Fanuc, Yaskawa) have healthcare adjacency.

### Entry/Exit Logic
- **Entry:** This is a structural multi-decade trade. Dollar-cost average into positions. Buy Japanese names on yen weakness (BOJ policy shifts). Buy ISRG/MDT/ABT on broad market corrections.
- **Entry (ETFs):** AGNG/OLD are ideal for steady accumulation. Add on 10%+ drawdowns.
- **Exit:** This is a hold-forever thesis. Only exit if: (a) major policy change reverses aging trend (unlikely), (b) breakthrough anti-aging technology disrupts the aging-as-disease thesis, (c) valuation becomes extreme (P/E >40 on slow-growth names).
- **Rebalance:** Shift from device makers to care providers if GLP-1 drugs reduce obesity-related surgical volumes (see Hypothesis 3 interaction).

### Expected Horizon
3-10+ years. This is the most certain macro trend in investing. Demographics are destiny. The aging of Japan/Europe/eventually China is irreversible. Position size accordingly -- smaller positions held longer.

American Water Works plans $19-20B in investment during 2026-2030. This combines regulatory mandate, climate change acceleration, and crumbling infrastructure into a secular growth thesis.

### Tickers

**Pure-Play Water (Long):**

Current ~$120, analyst PT $157.89 (31.7% upside). 9/10 analysts rate Buy. IIJA funding reaching implementation = multi-year tailwind.
- **AWK (American Water Works)** -- Largest US water utility. $19-20B investment planned 2026-2030. Regulated monopoly = predictable cash flows. Defensive growth.
- **ECL (Ecolab)** -- Water, hygiene, energy tech across 170 countries. Benefits from tightening water regulation globally. Premium valuation but premium business.
- **WMS (Advanced Drainage Systems)** -- Stormwater management infrastructure. Less well-known but directly tied to water infrastructure spending.

**Water-Adjacent Infrastructure (Long):**
- **AECOM (ACM)** -- Engineering/advisory for large-scale water projects. Strong project backlog. Benefits from federal infrastructure spending.
- **MWA (Mueller Water Products)** -- Water distribution pipes, valves, hydrants. Pure-play on municipal water infrastructure replacement.

**ETFs (Long):**

- **CGW (Invesco S&P Global Water Index ETF)** -- Global water exposure.

**Desalination / Advanced Tech (Speculative Long):**
- **ERII (Energy Recovery)** -- Pressure exchanger technology for desalination. As drought worsens, desalination demand grows.

### Entry/Exit Logic
- **Entry (XYL):** Current ~$120 is 12% below Feb 2026 levels after guidance-related sell-off. Attractive entry point. Buy at $115-125. The IIJA implementation tailwind hasn't fully materialized yet.
- **Entry (AWK):** Buy on any correction >10% from highs. This is a regulated utility -- valuation floor is high but so is reliability.
- **Entry (ETFs):** PHO/FIW for broad exposure. Accumulate quarterly.
- **Exit:** Trim XYL at analyst PT ($155-165). Only full exit if IIJA funding is rescinded or water regulation loosens (politically unlikely -- water is bipartisan). Exit ERII if desalination cost curves don't improve.
- **Stop loss:** 10% for individual names. ETFs are hold-through-volatility positions.

### Expected Horizon
12-36 months for XYL/AWK catalyst (IIJA implementation). 5-10+ years for the secular water scarcity thesis. Water is the "next oil" -- the scarcity is permanent and worsening.
