### Why the Stock Dropped 9%

1. **Q2 guidance missed.** Revenue guidance of $12.57B came in below consensus. EPS guidance of $0.78 vs $0.84 consensus.
2. **Operating margin decline.** Q2 margin projected at 32.6%, down from 34.1%, due to front-loaded content amortization.
3. **Reed Hastings departing.** Co-founder and board chairman will step down in June after 29 years. While the departure is amicable ("Netflix's greatness is so strong that I can now focus on new things"), it symbolizes an era ending.
4. **Sentiment vulnerable.** At $107.79 regular close, NFLX dropped to ~$98 in after-hours (-9%). The stock had run up into earnings.

### Why TSM Fell Despite the Beat

1. **Priced to perfection.** TSM rallied significantly into earnings. Options implied a 5% post-earnings move.
2. **Growth "not explosive enough."** Investors wanted TSMC to blow past its 30% annual growth target. Instead, guidance was "above 30%" -- strong but not euphoric.
3. **CapEx concerns.** $52-56B in spending is a large commitment. Questions about ROI timing.
4. **The ASML pattern.** ASML fell 4-6% earlier in the week despite beating and raising. TSM repeated the pattern.

**This is notable:** Both ASML and TSMC beat estimates, raised guidance, and fell. The semiconductor "sell the news" pattern may be signaling that AI chip enthusiasm is peaking in the near term, even as fundamentals remain excellent. This does NOT mean the AI thesis is broken -- it means the stocks have run too far ahead of even extraordinary earnings.

## 7. News & Events

### Trump Announces Israel-Lebanon 10-Day Ceasefire

President Trump announced Thursday that Israel and Lebanon have agreed to a 10-day ceasefire, effective 5 PM ET. Key details:

- **Direct talks:** Trump invited Israeli PM Netanyahu and Lebanese President Joseph Aoun to the White House. This would be the first direct talks between Israeli and Lebanese leaders in 34 years.
- **Israeli forces remain:** Netanyahu agreed to the ceasefire but said Israeli forces will stay in southern Lebanon.
- **Iran linkage:** Iran had said it would not negotiate with the US unless Israel entered a ceasefire in Lebanon. This removes that obstacle.
- **Nuclear angle:** Trump said Iran has "agreed to give us back the nuclear dust" from the B-2 bomber strikes, suggesting progress on the nuclear front.
- **Pakistan mediation:** Pakistan's army chief traveled to Tehran to secure a second round of US-Iran talks before the April 22 ceasefire deadline.

**Market read:** This is incrementally positive for peace and incrementally negative for oil. The Israel-Lebanon ceasefire removes one of Iran's preconditions for broader talks. However, the Strait of Hormuz blockade remains fully in place, and the core US-Iran deal remains unresolved. The April 22 deadline is the event that matters. Everything else is noise.

### Oil Reversal Higher

After falling $13 from Monday's $104 to Wednesday's $91, oil reversed sharply higher Thursday:
- Brent crude rose +3.4% toward $97
- WTI gained +2.3% above $93

The reversal was driven by doubts over the durability of the ceasefire and the fact that the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed. Oil was oversold on peace optimism and bounced.

### Reed Hastings Leaves Netflix

After 29 years, Netflix co-founder Reed Hastings will step down from the board in June (annual meeting June 4, 2026). He co-founded Netflix in 1997, served as CEO until 2023 (when he promoted Greg Peters), and has been chairman since. His departure is amicable but marks the definitive end of the founder era at one of the most transformative media companies in history.

### Economic Data: Mixed Picture

The Thursday data painted a conflicting picture:
- **Bullish:** Jobless claims fell to 207K (best since February). Philly Fed manufacturing surged to 26.7 (2.6x the 10.3 estimate).
- **Bearish:** Industrial production fell -0.5% (estimated +0.1%). Utilities, mining, and manufacturing all declined. Capacity utilization fell to 75.3%.
- **Net read:** The labor market is tight and manufacturing sentiment is improving, but actual production is contracting. This is consistent with a "wait-and-see" economy described in Wednesday's Beige Book.
