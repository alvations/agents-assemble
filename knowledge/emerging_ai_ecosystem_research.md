## 3. Unsloth AI

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2023 (Sydney, Australia; HQ in San Francisco)
- **Founders:** Daniel Han and Michael Han
- **Employees:** ~8
- **Status:** Private (very early stage)
- **Total Funding:** $500K (seed round, October 2024)
- **Downloads:** 2M+ monthly; 150M+ total
- **GitHub Stars:** 25,000+

### Funding History

| Round | Date | Amount | Key Investors |
|-------|------|--------|---------------|
| Seed (YC W24) | Oct 2024 | $500K | Y Combinator, Brightwing Capital, Orange Collective, Phosphor Capital |

### What They Do
Unsloth is an open-source framework for efficient LLM fine-tuning:
- **2x faster training** on a single NVIDIA GPU
- **70% less VRAM** usage with no accuracy loss
- Supports **500+ models** (Llama, DeepSeek, Qwen, Gemma, Mistral, GPT-oss, etc.)
- Custom CUDA kernels optimized for NVIDIA hardware (GeForce RTX through HGX B200)
- **Unsloth Studio:** No-code web UI for local model training (launched 2025-2026)

### Partnerships & Collaborations
- **Google:** Collaborated on Gemma model bug fixes
- **OpenAI:** Works with gpt-oss team
- **Meta:** Collaborated on Llama models
- **Mistral:** Debugging, testing for Devstral/Magistral models
- **NVIDIA:** Optimized for Blackwell GPUs (NVFP4 precision), featured on NVIDIA blog
  - RTX AI Garage collaboration
  - DGX Spark support

### Revenue Model
Currently primarily open-source/community-driven. Revenue model likely evolving:
1. Open-source core (free)
2. Unsloth Studio (premium features potential)
3. Enterprise support/consulting (likely future)
4. Cloud-hosted fine-tuning service (likely future)

### Supply Chain Dependencies
- **NVIDIA (NVDA):** Entirely dependent on NVIDIA GPUs for optimization
- **Google Colab/Kaggle:** Free tier fine-tuning platform
- **HuggingFace:** Model distribution platform
- **Meta, Google, Mistral:** Source of base models being fine-tuned

### Who Benefits If Unsloth Succeeds?
- **NVIDIA (NVDA):** More people fine-tuning = more GPU purchases (consumer AND enterprise)
- **Google (GOOGL):** More Colab/Kaggle usage; Gemma model adoption
- **Meta (META):** More Llama model adoption
- **Cloud providers (AMZN, GOOGL, MSFT):** More fine-tuning workloads
- **HuggingFace:** More models uploaded to Hub
- **Dell (DELL), Super Micro (SMCI):** More on-premise GPU server purchases for local fine-tuning

### Investment Thesis
Unsloth is extremely early-stage ($500K raised) but has massive community adoption. If they raise a Series A, their valuation could jump significantly. The key insight: **Unsloth makes the entire open-source fine-tuning ecosystem more efficient, driving GPU demand at every level.**

## 8. Strategy Thesis: Investing in the Open-Source/Emerging AI Ecosystem

### Core Thesis
The open-source AI ecosystem is experiencing exponential growth (1.2% to 30% of global AI usage in under 2 years). Unlike the closed-model companies (OpenAI, Anthropic), the open-source ecosystem creates value across a broad supply chain of publicly traded companies. The "picks and shovels" approach is even MORE relevant here because open-source AI requires MORE infrastructure, not less -- every company fine-tuning, hosting, and serving open models needs compute, storage, networking, and tooling.

### Three Investment Pillars

#### Pillar 1: "The Arms Dealers" (Highest Conviction)
**Thesis:** Every open-source model fine-tuned, hosted, or served requires GPUs and infrastructure.

**Core Holdings:**
- **NVDA** -- 92% GPU market share + direct investor in 4 of the featured companies
- **TSM** -- Fabricates ALL the chips; unavoidable beneficiary
- **ASML** -- 11% Mistral stake + enables all chip manufacturing
- **CRWV** -- Pure-play GPU cloud; $28B in major contracts since IPO

**Supporting Holdings:**
- AMD -- Alternative GPU supplier; investor in HuggingFace + Thinking Machines
- AVGO -- Custom ASIC growth (44.6% projected for 2026)
- DELL, SMCI -- Server hardware demand

#### Pillar 2: "The Platforms" (Distribution Layer)
**Thesis:** Open-source AI needs distribution, data, and cloud platforms to reach enterprise customers.

**Core Holdings:**
- **SNOW** -- Direct investor in Reka AI; $200M partnerships with Anthropic AND OpenAI; 7,700+ enterprise customers as distribution channel
- **ORCL** -- Reka AI infrastructure; $300B Stargate with OpenAI; $35B capex planned for 2026
- **AMZN** -- AWS is largest cloud; HuggingFace primary cloud partner

**Supporting Holdings:**
- GOOGL -- GCP + HuggingFace CDN + Gemma models
- MSFT -- Azure AI + eventual HuggingFace integration
- META -- Llama ecosystem creates massive downstream demand

#### Pillar 3: "The Enterprise Adopters" (Longer-Term Bet)
**Thesis:** Companies that successfully integrate open-source AI into their enterprise platforms gain a compounding advantage.

**Core Holdings:**
- **NOW** -- Investor in Thinking Machines; acquired Moveworks; agentic AI leader; 21% YoY growth
- **CRM** -- Investor in HuggingFace AND Mistral; Einstein AI platform
- **PLTR** -- AI platform running on open models; defense/enterprise contracts

### Key Risks
1. **Open-source commoditization:** If models become truly commodity, margins compress across the stack
2. **GPU oversupply:** If custom ASICs grow faster than expected, NVIDIA's margin could compress
3. **Regulatory risk:** EU AI Act and similar regulations could restrict open-source model distribution
4. **Concentration risk:** NVIDIA is present everywhere -- if they stumble, the whole ecosystem suffers
5. **Private company failure:** HuggingFace, Thinking Machines, Unsloth, or Reka could fail, reducing demand
6. **Valuation bubble:** Many AI companies are trading at extreme multiples
