- **Historical pattern:** Markets overreact to initial shock then recover. In 2022 Russia-Ukraine, Brent spiked 56% then faded. Early entry on escalation, trim into panic-buying tops.

## 2. Food Crisis & Fertilizer Shortage

### Context
Hormuz blockade cuts ~33% of seaborne urea and 20% of ammonia trade. Fertilizer prices spiked 26.2% (World Bank). Urea FOB Egypt jumped from $400-490 to ~$700/mt. Global food prices projected to rise 12-18% by end of 2026. 45M additional people face acute hunger.

### Tickers

**Fertilizer producers (direct beneficiaries):**

**Grain traders / Processors:**
| Ticker | Name | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| ADM | Archer-Daniels-Midland | Grain trading, processing (mixed: higher costs) |
| BG | Bunge Global | Grain origination, oilseed processing |

**Equipment (headwind risk):**
| Ticker | Name | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| DE | Deere & Company | Farmers may defer purchases if input costs soar |
| CNHI | CNH Industrial | Same headwind risk as DE |

### Strategy
- **Buy fertilizer stocks** when conflict disrupts Gulf shipping or sanctions hit fertilizer exports. NTR/CF benefit most from price spikes.
- **Caution on processors** (ADM, BG): higher raw material costs squeeze margins unless they can pass through.
- **Sell/trim** fertilizer on ceasefire signals or new supply agreements.

## 3. Agriculture & Livestock Commodity ETFs

### Context
UN FAO reported food prices rising in Feb 2026. Corn, wheat, soybeans strengthening. 30% of global fertilizers originate from Gulf region. MOO broke out in early 2026 but still below 2022 highs.

### Tickers

| Ticker | Name | Exposure | ER |
|--------|------|----------|------|
| MOO | VanEck Agribusiness ETF | Equities: fertilizers, seeds, equipment, livestock | 0.54% |
| DBA | Invesco DB Agriculture Fund | Futures: corn, soybeans, wheat, sugar, cattle, hogs | 0.93% |
| WEAT | Teucrium Wheat Fund | Pure wheat futures | 1.00% |
| CORN | Teucrium Corn Fund | Pure corn futures | 1.00% |
| SOYB | Teucrium Soybean Fund | Pure soybean futures | 1.00% |
| COW | iPath Livestock ETN | Cattle + hog futures | 0.45% |
| RJA | Elements Rogers Agri ETN | Broad agriculture basket | 0.75% |

### Strategy
- **MOO** for equity exposure with ~2.2% yield; diversified across the ag value chain.
- **DBA** for direct commodity futures exposure (corn, soy, wheat, livestock).
- **WEAT/CORN/SOYB** for targeted single-crop bets when specific supply disruptions hit.
- **Buy signal:** Fundstrat's Mark Newton notes "agriculture's rally is kicking into gear as US equity rally wobbles" -- rotation trade into hard assets.
- **Timing:** Buy ahead of planting season (Mar-May) when fertilizer shortages bite yields. Sell into harvest (Sep-Nov) if supply normalizes.
- **Risk:** Contango in futures-based ETFs (DBA, WEAT) erodes returns over time. Prefer MOO for longer holds.
