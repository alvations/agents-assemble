## 4. Warren Buffett's Value Investing Principles

- **Moat types**: Cost advantages, intangible assets (brands), network effects, switching costs, efficient scale
- **Core rule**: "Buy wonderful companies at fair prices, not fair companies at wonderful prices"
- **Quality metrics**: Consistent earnings, high ROE, low debt, wise capital allocation
- **Portfolio**: Concentrated (10-15 positions), hold forever unless thesis breaks
- **BRK top holdings**: AAPL, BAC, KO, AXP, OXY, CVX, MCO, AMZN, V, MA
- **Key insight**: Buffett's alpha comes from quality + leverage (insurance float), not stock picking alone

Sources:

## 5. Dividend Growth Investing (DRIP/Compounding)

- **Power of DRIP**: $10K at 8% dividend + 4% growth + 5% price appreciation = $32,469 in 10 years (CAGR ~12.5%)
- **20-year DRIP advantage**: ~75% more assets vs taking cash dividends
- **Dividend Aristocrats** (NOBL): 25+ consecutive years of dividend increases
- **Dividend Kings**: 50+ consecutive years of dividend increases
- **Key ETFs**: SCHD, VIG, NOBL, DVY, HDV
- **Dollar cost averaging**: DRIP naturally buys more shares when prices are low
- **Tax efficiency**: DRIP in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid dividend tax drag

Sources:
- [FasterCapital - DRIP Compounding Guide](https://fastercapital.com/content/Dividend-Reinvestment-Plan--DRIP---Compounding-Wealth-with-DRIPs--A-Long-Term-Investor-s-Guide.html)

## 6. Equal Weight vs Market Cap Weight (S&P 500)

- **Long-term outperformance**: Equal weight beat cap-weight by 1.05% annually (to 2023); +63bps/year since 1990
- **Total return difference**: 742% cumulative over full period since 1990
- **Earnings growth**: Equal weight grew earnings 12% annualized vs 9% for cap-weight (since 2009)
- **Recent underperformance**: Mag-7 concentration caused 13.1% underperformance over past 2 years (2023-2025)
- **Key insight**: After peaks in market concentration, equal weight tends to outperform
- **ETF**: RSP (Invesco S&P 500 Equal Weight)
- **Trade-offs**: Higher turnover, higher expense ratio, more volatile during recessions

Sources:

## 7. Joel Greenblatt's Magic Formula

- **Two factors**: High earnings yield (cheap) + High return on capital (quality)
- **1988-2009 CAGR**: 23.8% vs 9.6% for S&P 500
- **2003-2015 CAGR**: 11.4% vs 8.7% S&P 500
- **2016-2025 CAGR**: 10.7% vs 9.5% benchmark
- **International**: Beats French market by 5-9%/year; Hong Kong by 6-15%/year
- **Method**: Rank all stocks by earnings yield + ROIC, buy top 20-30, hold 1 year, repeat
- **Key insight**: Works globally but with higher volatility; must commit to 3+ year horizon

Sources:
- [Wikipedia - Magic Formula Investing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magic_formula_investing)

## 8. Quality Factor Investing

- **MSCI Quality Factor outperformance**: +2.6% annually over broad market (1975-2018)
- **Key metrics**: High ROE, stable earnings, low debt (leverage)
- **Defensive behavior**: Outperforms by 23-31 bps/month during high-volatility periods
- **Long-term consistency**: Over 3-year and 5-year rolling periods, quality outperforms more frequently
- **ETFs**: QUAL (iShares MSCI USA Quality), DGRW (WisdomTree Quality Dividend Growth)
- **Key insight**: Profitability (ROE) is the strongest and most persistent quality signal

Sources:

## 9. GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price)

- **Philosophy**: Hybrid of value + growth, pioneered by Peter Lynch (29% CAGR over 13 years at Magellan)
- **Key metric**: PEG ratio (P/E / EPS growth rate); target PEG < 1.5
- **Performance**: More consistent returns than pure growth or pure value across market cycles
- **Rate resilience**: Outperforms pure growth during rate hikes (strong current cash flows)
- **Key insight**: Avoid stocks at 50-60x earnings; look for 15-25x with 15-25% growth

Sources:
- [iShares - GARP Investing](https://www.ishares.com/us/insights/garp-investing-etf-valuations)
- [Morgan Stanley - GARP](https://www.morganstanley.com/articles/growth-investing-value-combined-garp)

## 10. Dogs of the Dow

- **Method**: Buy 10 highest-yielding Dow Jones stocks on Jan 1, hold 1 year, repeat
- **1957-2003 CAGR**: 14.3% vs 11% for Dow average
- **2012-2022**: 11.6% annually (slightly trailed S&P 500)
- **20-year return**: 10.8% (matched Dow exactly)
- **Small Dogs variant**: Top 5 lowest-priced of the 10 Dogs; 9.3% CAGR since 2000
- **Key insight**: Works as mean-reversion strategy; struggles during growth/momentum markets
