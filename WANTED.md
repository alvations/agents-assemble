## Others

EQS: The Equity Screener, used to find stocks meeting specific financial criteria.
DES: Comprehensive security description and summary page.
FA: Financial Analysis; pulls consolidated financial statements and fundamental data.
ANR: Analyst Recommendations and price target summaries.
HDS: Holders Dashboard; shows institutional and insider ownership.
CACS: Corporate Actions Calendar (dividends, splits, mergers).
MGMT: Management profiles and executive backgrounds.
EE: Earnings Estimates and consensus forecasts.
ERN: Historical earnings summary and surprises.
RELS: Shows related securities, including subsidiaries and parents.
SPLC: Supply Chain analysis to track a company's customers and suppliers.
RV: Relative Value; compares a stock to its direct peers across various metrics.
WACC: Calculates a company’s Weighted Average Cost of Capital.
BETA: Calculates the historical beta of a security against an index.
CF: Accesses official company regulatory filings (SEC, etc.). 
SRCH: Highly customizable bond search engine.
YAS: Yield and Spread Analysis for pricing fixed-income securities.
GC: Graph Curves; visualizes and compares yield curves over time.
CRPR: Credit Rating Profile showing issuer ratings from major agencies.
DDIS: Debt Distribution; displays the maturity schedule of an issuer's debt.
ALLQ: Aggregates dealer-contributed prices for corporate bonds.
CAST: Capital Structure visualization of a company's debt and equity layers.
WIRP: World Interest Rate Probability; shows market expectations for central bank hikes/cuts.
SOVR: Sovereign CDS monitor for tracking national default risk.
NIM: New Issue Monitor for tracking upcoming and recently priced bond offerings. 
GP: Standard price graph for historical price and volume trends.
GIP: Intraday price graph for tracking security movement within the day.
HS: Historical Spread; compares the price relationship between two securities.
COMP: Comparative returns analysis against benchmarks or other tickers.
RRG: Relative Rotation Graphs to visualize sector or stock momentum.
GE: Price/Earnings graph for visual valuation analysis. 
N: The main news menu for global headlines.
READ: A list of the most-read news stories on the terminal.
BI: Bloomberg Intelligence; deep industry-level research and dashboards.
RES: Search for research reports from over 1,500 sources.
MA: Mergers & Acquisitions database to track global deal flow.
TNI: News filter to create custom news feeds based on keywords

WEI: World Equity Indices. Used at the start of shows like Bloomberg Surveillance or The Open to provide a snapshot of global market performance. 
WIRP: World Interest Rate Probability. A "fan favorite" during Fed meetings to show the market-implied odds of rate hikes or cuts.
TOP: Top News Headlines. Frequently referred to as the "front page" of the terminal where the day's biggest stories—and TV segment topics—are listed. 
GP: Price Graph. The most basic chart command; anchors use it to show the "key events" or "spikes" in a stock's price that are currently making headlines.



## AI-Powered Features

- **Cortex AI Custom Scans:** Users can write custom indicators or screeners using natural language prompts without needing to know "mnemonics" or coding. Example: "Find me all S&P 500 stocks with RSI below 30 and price above SMA200 that have beaten earnings 3 quarters in a row" → instant results.

- **Options Profit/Loss Visualizer:** A beginner-friendly tool that simulates potential returns and risks for options trades before they are placed. Shows payoff diagrams, break-even points, max profit/loss, probability of profit, and Greeks — all with simple slider inputs (strike, expiry, direction). Then one-click to execute via Public.com API.
